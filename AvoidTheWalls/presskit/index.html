Description

Goodwolf Studio is an independent game developer committed to creating games where the gameplay enables the player to uncover a deeper story. Its founders and members, Kevin Glaap and Zein Okko, met each other at the Cologne Game Lab where they are both pursuing their Master's degrees in Game Development and Research. Narrative games like "Thomas Was Alone" or "Tales from the Borderlands" inspired them to create epic, story-driven projects, and they began developing games together in 2014.

History

Zein Okko

Zein's greatest passion is writing and telling stories. He has been working as a game and narrative designer since 2011 after finishing his Bachelor of Arts in Media Design. Before founding Goodwolf Studio, he was working on the award-winning "Typoman" for the Wii U as a narrative designer and animator.

Kevin Glaap

Kevin has been developing interactive websites and games since his early childhood. This passion accompanied him through all his school years which finally made him study computer science at the Rheinischen Friedrich-Wilhelms-University in Bonn. After that he attended the master program "Game Development and Research" at the Cologne Game Lab of the TH Köln. Also he has several years of experience as software developer, though his main interest was always game development. Here he specializes on game design and user experience.

Description

What would you do if you found yourself trapped on an eerie space station with nothing but a computer? Play as the hacker Alex and help your partner Sam uncover the horrible truth of the facility. It doesn't take long until you have to ask yourself who Alex really is and if your reality is what it seems to be. Gather information, navigate, talk and hack your way through five chapters of this atmospheric, fully-voiced text adventure. Code 7 is being developed and published by Goodwolf Studio for PC, Mac and Linux.

History

Code 7 started as an one-week prototype during the Master Studies at the Cologne Game Lab and was published for free in early 2015. The idea quickly developed to become an episodic text adventure with professional voice actors. In September 2016 a remake of the original prototype (Episode 0: Allocation) has been released, together with the launch of the Kickstarter campaign. The developers, Kevin Glaap and Zein Okko, successfully raised ~16.000€ to continue working on the four upcoming episodes.

Features

  • a modern, thrilling text adventure
  • fully-voiced dialogue
  • atmospheric music and sound
  • easy keyboard controls with auto-complete help
  • multiple endings
